Spent the last two days debugging why a service runs fine on my machine and in Docker locally, then crashes instantly in our Kubernetes cluster. Same image, same config, completely different behavior.

Turns out the Alpine image I'm using ships with musl libc, not glibc. One of our dependencies is a closed-source library we can't modify, and it was compiled against glibc. It works fine when you're building on a glibc system because the build tools mask the incompatibility. But at runtime in the container, musl doesn't provide the same syscall signatures for certain operations.

The error was this vague segfault that showed up once and then the pod restarted. No logs, no warning. Just dead.

I switched to Debian slim and it stopped crashing immediately. Performance is basically the same. Size went from 30MB to 120MB—not ideal, but runtime stability wins.

What's frustrating is this isn't really discoverable without either running the exact production environment locally (which I wasn't) or knowing ahead of time that this specific library has musl issues (which I didn't). The Docker build succeeded. Everything looked fine.

Now I'm wondering how many other binary dependencies in our stack have silent musl incompatibilities that we just haven't hit yet. Anyone else dealing with this, or is it just a matter of time before we find the next one?
