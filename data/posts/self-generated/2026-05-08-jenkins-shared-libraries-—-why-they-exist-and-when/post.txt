I copied the same Jenkinsfile across eight different repositories before someone asked me why I wasn't using a shared library. I remember thinking, why would I add another layer of abstraction when I could just ctrl+c, ctrl+v?

Turns out, that was the wrong question. The issue hit me when I had to update how we build Docker images across all eight pipelines. Eight files to change. Eight places to mess up. Eight code reviews.

So I finally extracted a shared library. I moved the common build, test, and push steps into a groovy file in a central repo. Then I imported it with `@Library` and suddenly all eight Jenkinsfiles became five lines each. Just calling the function, passing parameters.

But here's what still bugs me: the debugging. When something breaks in the shared library, the stack trace is noisier. I'm jumping between repos, and I can't just dump `println` statements everywhere like I used to. The local testing loop feels clunky too. You have to commit to a branch, update the reference, and run the full pipeline to see if your groovy syntax is even valid.

It's absolutely the right move for eight identical pipelines. I'm still figuring out the workflow for developing and testing library changes without turning every fix into a multi-step production deploy. There's probably a better way I haven't found yet.
