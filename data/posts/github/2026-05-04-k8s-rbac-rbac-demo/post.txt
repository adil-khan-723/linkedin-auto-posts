Something bit me this week that I thought I had figured out years ago: Kubernetes RBAC.

We had a new service account that needed read access to pods in one namespace. Simple enough. I added the Role, created the RoleBinding, applied it. Ran a quick test. Access denied. Spent an embarrassing amount of time verifying the Role was correct before I thought to check which namespace the RoleBinding was actually created in. Wrong namespace. Classic.

The thing that gets me is how quiet RBAC failures are. No "you bound this to the wrong namespace." Just access denied, go figure it out. I ended up pulling together a small demo to make sense of it, roles, bindings, service accounts, and how namespace scope affects each one, mostly so I'd stop second-guessing myself every time I saw a 403.

What I still don't fully have wired: how to get visibility into what a given service account can actually do at runtime without running auth can-i queries by hand for every resource combination. There's a whole observability angle here that feels like it should be solved but every tool I've tried has some catch. Still looking.
